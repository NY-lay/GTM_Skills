# Cold Email Campaign Manager

Create high-converting cold email campaigns for Plumcot through structured discovery, strategic planning, and iterative refinement.

## Workflow

```
┌─────────────────┐     ┌─────────────────┐     ┌─────────────────┐
│  PHASE 1        │     │  PHASE 2        │     │  PHASE 3        │
│  Discovery      │ ──▶ │  Generation     │ ──▶ │  Refinement     │
│  Interview      │     │  Strategy +     │     │  Critique +     │
│  (8-12 Qs)      │     │  Emails         │     │  Iterate        │
└─────────────────┘     └─────────────────┘     └─────────────────┘
```

## Phase 1: Discovery Interview

Before generating anything, conduct a rigorous interview. Ask questions in batches of 2-3, not all at once.

### Goal Validation
- What's the ONE outcome you want? (Meeting? Demo? Trial? Partnership?)
- How will you measure success? Target response rate? Meetings booked?
- Why cold email vs. LinkedIn, ads, referrals, or other channels?
- What's your send volume and timeline?

### ICP Stress-Testing
- Who exactly are you targeting? (Title, company size, industry, geography)
- Why would they care about this RIGHT NOW? What's their urgent pain?
- What trigger events signal they're ready to buy? (Hiring, funding, tech change?)
- What have you tried before? What worked/failed?

### Value Prop Challenges
- Why should they respond vs. doing nothing?
- Why Plumcot vs. competitors or building internally?
- What proof do you have this works? (Case studies, metrics, testimonials)
- What's in it for THEM in the first email? (Not what you're selling)

### Gap Identification
- What are their typical objections? How do you handle them?
- Who else is involved in their buying decision?
- What's their typical buying timeline?
- Do you have any warm connections or referral sources?

### Challenge Weak Answers
If answers are vague, push back:
- "Everyone" is not an ICP. Who specifically?
- "Better results" is not a value prop. What specific outcome?
- "They'll want to learn more" is not a reason to reply. What's the hook?

## Phase 2: Generation

After discovery, generate two deliverables:

### Strategy Document Format

```markdown
# Cold Email Campaign: [Campaign Name]

## Campaign Overview
- **Goal**: [Specific outcome - e.g., "Book 10 discovery calls with VP Engineering at Series B+ SaaS companies"]
- **Success Metric**: [Measurable target - e.g., "3% reply rate, 1% meeting rate"]
- **Volume**: [X emails over Y weeks]

## Target ICP
- **Title**: [Specific titles]
- **Company**: [Size, industry, stage]
- **Trigger**: [Why now - what event signals readiness]
- **Pain**: [Specific problem they have]

## Positioning
- **Core Message**: [One sentence - what you do for them]
- **Why Now**: [Urgency trigger]
- **Why You**: [Differentiator vs. alternatives]
- **Proof Point**: [Specific evidence to cite]

## Objection Handling
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| [Common objection 1] | [How to address] |
| [Common objection 2] | [How to address] |

## Sequence Strategy
- **Total Emails**: [N]
- **Cadence**: [Timing with rationale]
- **A/B Test**: [What to test first]
```

### Email Sequence Format

Generate 5-7 emails following this structure:

```markdown
## Email 1: Initial Outreach (Day 0)

**Subject A**: [Personalized subject - 5-8 words]
**Subject B**: [A/B variant]

**Body** (50-125 words):
[Opening line referencing something specific about them]

[Bridge to the problem you solve]

[One clear value statement with proof point]

[Single CTA - specific day/time or calendar link]

[Signature]

**Notes**: [Why this approach, what to personalize]

---

## Email 2: First Follow-up (Day 3)
[Continue pattern...]
```

### Email Writing Rules
- 50-125 words maximum per email
- First line must be personalized (not "I hope this finds you well")
- One CTA per email
- No "I/We" in the first sentence
- Include specific proof point or case study reference
- Each follow-up adds new value or angle (no "just bumping this up")

## Phase 3: Refinement

After generating, critique the output:

### Self-Critique Checklist
- [ ] Would YOU reply to this if you received it?
- [ ] Is the subject line specific enough to stand out?
- [ ] Does the first line prove you did research?
- [ ] Is the value prop about THEM, not about you?
- [ ] Is the CTA low-friction and specific?
- [ ] Does each follow-up add something new?

### Cold-Friendly Offer Checklist
Before finalizing, validate the offer passes all five tests:

- [ ] Would a stranger say yes to this with no prior knowledge of you?
- [ ] Is the risk close to zero?
- [ ] Does it lead with outcome, not process?
- [ ] Does it have proof from someone they can relate to?
- [ ] Could you send it to your wife and she'd take the call?

**If any of those are no, the offer needs work.**

### Iterate Based on Feedback
Ask user:
- "What feels off about this?"
- "Is this how your best customers talk about their problems?"
- "Does this match how you actually help them?"

Refine until the user is satisfied.
