# Cold Email Strategy Framework

## Subject Lines

### Best Practices
- Personalize with recipient's name (up to 22% higher open rates)
- Avoid spam triggers
- Keep short and concise
- Spark curiosity with intrigue
- A/B test different approaches

### Examples
- "{Name}, This Strategy Will Increase Your Productivity!"
- "Do You Want to Increase Your Profile This Quarter, {Name}?"
- Personalized + relevant detail from research

## Email Personalization

### Strategies
1. **Use Their Name**: Start with recipient's name for personal touch
2. **Research**: Learn about their business and interests to reference
3. **Include Specific Details**: Mention designation, company name, relevant findings
4. **Show Genuine Interest**: Reference their LinkedIn, recent blog post, company news

## Email Length

**Optimal**: 50-125 words receive highest response rates
- Busy professionals receive ~129 emails/day
- Capture attention within first 15-20 seconds
- Focus on essential details only
- Be direct and clear

## Email Structure

### Good Email Pattern
1. Personal greeting with name
2. Specific observation about them/their company (shows research)
3. Bridge to how you can help
4. Clear value proposition
5. Single, clear CTA
6. Professional signature

### Bad Email Pattern (Avoid)
- Generic intro ("I am X from Y company")
- Self-focused ("We specialize in...", "We were awarded...")
- Vague value prop
- No personalization
- Multiple CTAs

## Value Proposition

### Strategies
1. **State the Reason**: Clearly explain why you're reaching out
2. **Highlight Benefits**: Show how you add value to their business
3. **Showcase Advantages**: Explain benefits of working with you
4. **Use Case Studies**: Provide examples of results for similar organizations

## Call to Action (CTA)

### Best Practices
- Single focus (one clear CTA)
- Clear and urgent
- Action-oriented
- Specific time/date when possible

### Examples
- "Can we connect this Friday to discuss?"
- "You can sign up for our 7-day free trial here"
- "Are you available for a quick call? Let's connect."
- "Let me know if I can get 15 minutes this Tuesday"

## Follow-Up Sequence

### Timing (7-touchpoint sequence)
| Email | Day | Purpose |
|-------|-----|---------|
| Initial | Day 0 | First outreach |
| Follow-up 1 | Day 3 | Quick check-in |
| Follow-up 2 | Day 7 | Add new value/angle |
| Follow-up 3 | Day 14 | Different approach |
| Follow-up 4 | Day 21 | Social proof |
| Follow-up 5 | Day 28 | Urgency/scarcity |
| Follow-up 6 | Day 38 | Breakup email |

### Sequence Types
1. **Time-Based**: Regular intervals regardless of engagement
2. **Intent-Based**: Triggered by signals (opens, clicks, replies)

### Follow-up Rules
- Keep personalized and concise like initial email
- Each email should add new value or angle
- Don't just "bump" or "check in" without substance

## Deliverability Metrics

### Targets
- **Bounce Rate**: Keep below 3%
- **Delivery Rate**: Aim for 95%+
- **Spam Rate**: Keep below 0.01%

### Impact
High deliverability = more inbox placement = higher engagement = better ROI

## Optimization Areas

### 1. Timing
- Tech companies: Monday mornings often best
- HR professionals: Afternoons may be more effective
- A/B test to find optimal times for your audience

### 2. Email Length
- Test various lengths (50-125 word sweet spot)
- Analyze response rates by content type
- Adjust for concise, clear, compelling copy

### 3. Value Proposition
If high opens but low replies:
- Restructure offer to align with ICP
- Refine pitch to address specific pain points
- Enhance CTA to be more compelling

## Key Takeaways
1. Perfect timing (align with target's schedule)
2. Optimize length (concise, 50-125 words)
3. Enhance relevance (value prop + CTA tailored to ICP)
4. Follow up persistently (7-8 touchpoints)
5. Measure and iterate (track metrics, A/B test)
